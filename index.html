Child-Friendly Restaurants in London

About Tiny Diners

When eating out with children, it’s all too easy to plump for the safe option, and simply head to your local restaurant chain where the kid’s menu is the usual uninspiring and unhealthy blend of chicken and chips, pizza or spaghetti Bolognese and the adult menu is often underwhelming and no different to the mid week meals that you whip up yourself at home.

Here at Tiny Diners, we are on a mission to help families step outside the safety zone and find great places to eat in London. These restaurants will offer a welcoming environment for young children as well as good quality food and thoughtful menus.

So, here we bring you a collection of reviews for a range of restaurants – from our favourite local spots to child-friendly, central London eateries. It’s time for you to enjoy eating out again with your tiny diners!

Reviews

Chapters - Blackheath, SE3

Chapters in Blackheath Village, offers a lovely, family-friendly environment. The staff are welcoming and great with the children. The kids menu is really well thought-out and offers plenty of freshly cooked, healthy options. They have now also launched the Chapters Kidz Club which offers a range of rewards specially designed for families - additional reassurance of their family-friendly credentials.
